Your questions, answered

We hear these questions in every conversation. Here are the answers.

What about union concerns?
Data is anonymised by design. Supervisors see risk levels, not biometric data. No individual is ever named on an organisational report. The system is built around special category data rules. It is a wellbeing tool, not a surveillance tool.

Drivers own their own data. They see their own app. The system gives drivers visibility into their own fatigue patterns, and that transparency builds trust. Edinburgh Trams and Irish Rail both run BaselineNC with full union support.
Can I justify the cost?
One fatigue-related incident costs an average of £500,000 in direct costs, legal fees, and reputational damage. BaselineNC costs less than a coffee a day per person. £1,000 per person per year.

Beyond incident prevention: fatigued employees cost organisations up to £3,730 per year in lost productivity. The system pays for itself through reduced absenteeism, reduced presenteeism, and better shift planning alone.
What do we actually do when we find a problem?
The system flags trends, not individuals. You get time to act, not a crisis to manage. A shift change, a rest break, a conversation. The system gives you options, not obligations.

When 80% of alerts come from 4 drivers, the answer is rarely disciplinary. It is usually a scheduling fix, a route adjustment, or a wellbeing conversation. BaselineNC gives you the data to make informed, proportionate decisions.
